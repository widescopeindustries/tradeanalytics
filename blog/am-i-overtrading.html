Am I Overtrading? 5 Signs You're Taking Too Many Trades

January 29, 2026 · 6 min read · Trading Psychology

Overtrading is the silent killer of trading accounts. It's not dramatic like a single blown trade — it's death by a thousand cuts. Commissions, spreads, bad fills, and emotional fatigue slowly drain your account while you convince yourself you're "staying active."

Here are 5 data-driven signs that you're overtrading — and what to do about it.

Sign #1: Your Win Rate Drops After Trade #3

Look at your performance by trade sequence each day. Your first 2-3 trades often have a higher win rate than trades 4, 5, 6+.

Why it happens: Early trades are planned. Later trades are often forced — either chasing, revenge trading, or fighting boredom.

Check your data: Compare win rate and profit factor for first 3 trades vs all trades after. If there's a big drop, you have your answer.

Sign #2: You Trade More on Losing Days

Count your trades on green days vs red days. If you're taking significantly more trades on losing days, you're revenge trading.

Red flag: If your losing days have 50%+ more trades than your winning days, overtrading is actively destroying your account.

Why it happens: Losses trigger the urge to "make it back." Each trade digs the hole deeper.

Sign #3: Your P&L Per Trade is Declining

Calculate your average P&L per trade. If it's shrinking over time (or barely covers commissions), you're diluting your edge with low-quality trades.

The math: If your edge is $15/trade on quality setups, but you're averaging $3/trade overall, you're taking too many marginal trades.

Check your data: Rank your trades by quality (if you track setups) or by profit. Your top 50% of trades likely account for 100%+ of your profits.

Sign #4: You Trade During "Dead" Hours

Look at when you're trading. If you're active during lunch hours (11am-2pm) or late in the day when you're tired, you're probably forcing it.

Why it happens: Boredom. FOMO. The need to feel productive. None of these are good reasons to trade.

Check your data: Calculate profit factor by hour. If certain hours are below 1.0, you're literally paying to trade during those times.

Sign #5: Your Commissions Are a Significant % of P&L

Add up your total commissions and compare to your gross P&L. If commissions are eating more than 20% of gross profits, you're trading too much.

The math: 100 trades at $1 commission = $100. If your gross profit is $400, commissions took 25%. That's too high.

Elite traders: Often keep commissions under 10% of gross by being selective.

How to Fix Overtrading

  1. Set a daily trade limit. Start with 3 trades max. Increase only if you're consistently profitable.
  2. Define your A+ setups. If it's not an A+ setup, don't take it. Period.
  3. Stop after 2 consecutive losses. Walk away for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Track trade quality. Rate each trade 1-5 before you enter. Only take 4s and 5s.
  5. Review your data weekly. Know your numbers. They don't lie.
