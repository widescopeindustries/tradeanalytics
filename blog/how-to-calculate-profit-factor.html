How to Calculate Profit Factor (And Why It Matters More Than Win Rate)

January 29, 2026 ¬∑ 6 min read ¬∑ Trading Metrics

Every new trader obsesses over win rate. "I won 70% of my trades!" sounds impressive ‚Äî until you realize those wins averaged $50 and the losses averaged $200.

That's why professional traders focus on profit factor instead. It tells you the full story.

What Is Profit Factor?

Profit factor is the ratio of your gross profits to your gross losses. It answers a simple question: for every dollar you lose, how many dollars do you make back?

Profit Factor = Gross Profits √∑ Gross Losses

A profit factor above 1.0 means you're profitable. Below 1.0 means you're losing money.

How to Calculate Profit Factor: Step by Step

  1. Add up all your winning trades. If you had 3 winners of $100, $150, and $75, your gross profit is $325.
  2. Add up all your losing trades. If you had 2 losers of $80 and $120, your gross loss is $200.
  3. Divide profits by losses. $325 √∑ $200 = 1.625 profit factor.

Why Profit Factor Beats Win Rate

Consider two traders:

  • Trader A: 80% win rate, average win $25, average loss $150. Profit factor: 0.67
  • Trader B: 40% win rate, average win $300, average loss $75. Profit factor: 2.67

Trader A wins most trades but loses money. Trader B loses most trades but makes serious profit. Win rate is meaningless without context.

How to Improve Your Profit Factor

  1. Cut losers faster. Reduce average loss by using tighter stops or exiting at first sign of trouble.
  2. Let winners run. Increase average win by using trailing stops instead of fixed targets.
  3. Filter low-quality setups. Some setups have better profit factors than others. Find yours.
  4. Analyze by time of day. Your profit factor at 10am might be 2.0 while at 2pm it's 0.8.
