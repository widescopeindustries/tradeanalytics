Trading Expectancy Formula: Calculate Your True Edge

January 29, 2026 · 5 min read · Trading Metrics

Expectancy answers the most important question in trading: how much money can you expect to make (or lose) on each trade?

It combines win rate and risk/reward into a single number that tells you whether your trading system actually has an edge.

The Expectancy Formula

There are two ways to calculate expectancy:

Simple Method: Expectancy = (Win Rate × Avg Win) - (Loss Rate × Avg Loss)

Or equivalently:

R-Multiple Method: Expectancy = (Win Rate × Avg R-Win) - (Loss Rate × 1R)

The result tells you your expected profit per trade in dollars (or R-multiples).

Example Calculation

Let's say over 100 trades:

  • Win rate: 45% (45 wins, 55 losses)
  • Average winning trade: $150
  • Average losing trade: $80
Expectancy = (0.45 × $150) - (0.55 × $80)
Expectancy = $67.50 - $44.00
Expectancy = $23.50 per trade

This means on average, you make $23.50 every time you take a trade. Over 100 trades, that's $2,350 in expected profit.

What's a Good Expectancy?

  • Negative expectancy: You're losing money. Stop trading this system.
  • $0 - $10 per trade: Barely break-even after commissions. Needs work.
  • $10 - $30 per trade: Solid edge. Scalable with more capital.
  • $30+ per trade: Strong edge. Make sure sample size is large enough.

Note: These numbers depend on your position size. A better way is to express expectancy in R-multiples (expected R per trade).

Expectancy in R-Multiples

If you risk $100 per trade (1R), and your expectancy is 0.35R, you expect to make $35 per trade risked.

Expectancy (R) = (Win Rate × Avg Winner in R) - (Loss Rate × 1)

Good R-expectancy benchmarks:

  • 0.1R - 0.2R: Modest edge (common for high-frequency strategies)
  • 0.2R - 0.5R: Strong edge
  • 0.5R+: Exceptional (verify with large sample size)

Why Expectancy Matters More Than Win Rate

Consider two traders:

  • Trader A: 70% win rate, avg win $40, avg loss $100. Expectancy = -$2/trade (losing!)
  • Trader B: 35% win rate, avg win $200, avg loss $60. Expectancy = +$31/trade (profitable!)

Win rate alone is meaningless. Expectancy is truth.

How to Improve Your Expectancy

  1. Increase win rate: Better entry criteria, patience for A+ setups.
  2. Increase average winner: Let winners run, use trailing stops.
  3. Decrease average loser: Tighter stops, cut losers faster.
  4. Filter by setup type: Some setups have higher expectancy. Trade those more.
