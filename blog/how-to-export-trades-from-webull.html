How to Export Your Trades from Webull for Analysis

January 29, 2026 · 4 min read · How-To

Want to analyze your Webull trading performance? First, you need to export your trade history. Here's exactly how to do it.

Method 1: Export from Webull Desktop App

The desktop app offers the most complete export options.

  1. Open the Webull Desktop App
    Download from webull.com if you haven't already. Log in with your credentials.
  2. Go to Orders → History
    Click on "Orders" in the bottom menu, then select the "History" tab at the top.
  3. Set Your Date Range
    Use the date picker to select the period you want to analyze. You can export up to 1 year at a time.
  4. Click the Export Button
    Look for the download/export icon (usually top right of the history table). Select "Export to CSV".
  5. Save the File
    Choose where to save your CSV file. Name it something descriptive like "webull-trades-jan-2026.csv".

Method 2: Export from Webull Web

  1. Log in to app.webull.com
  2. Navigate to Account → History → Orders
  3. Set your date range
  4. Click the download icon to export as CSV
Pro tip: The desktop app typically provides more detailed data than the mobile app or web version. Use desktop for the best export.

Method 3: Request from Webull Support

For trade history older than 1 year, or if you need more detailed records:

  1. Contact Webull support through the app
  2. Request a detailed trade history export
  3. Specify the date range you need
  4. They'll email you the data (may take 1-3 business days)

What Data You'll Get

A typical Webull CSV export includes:

  • Symbol: The stock/option ticker
  • Side: Buy or Sell
  • Quantity: Number of shares/contracts
  • Price: Fill price
  • Time: When the order was filled
  • Order Type: Market, Limit, Stop, etc.
  • Status: Filled, Cancelled, etc.

Next Step: Analyze Your Trades

Once you have your CSV, you can:

  • Import into Excel/Google Sheets for manual analysis
  • Upload to TradeAnalyticsAI for automated AI-powered insights
  • Use a trading journal like Tradervue or TradeZella

Troubleshooting

Can't find the export button? Make sure you're in the History tab, not the Open Orders tab. The export option only appears for completed orders.

CSV looks weird? Open it in Google Sheets instead of Excel. Excel sometimes misformats dates and times.

Missing trades? Check your date range. Also, some paper trading data may not export the same as live trades.
